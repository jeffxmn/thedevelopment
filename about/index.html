About the site

The Development is a website and blog dedicated to sharing stories about people and their websites, in interview form as inspired by The Setup.

Interviews

To keep things interesting and refrain from abundant amounts of interviews, new interviews will be posted twice a week on Mondays and Wednesdays.

If you'd like to get yourself interviewed on the website, it's easy! Email us at thedevelopment.interviews@gmail.com with your (carefully edited and spellchecked!) answers to these questions:

  1. Let's get to know you first. Tell us about yourself.
  2. Cool, so what's the story behind your website and its design?
  3. What part of your website is your favourite, and why?
  4. Is there anything you wish you knew when you first started building your website?
  5. Technologies, languages, frameworks, or libraries? * [Optional]
  6. Any upcoming changes we should look out for?

We'd also need some meta information:

  1. Your Website URL, of course!
  2. A position or identifier you want to use (like, "Web developer in Toronto")
  3. Your Twitter handle, or if you don't use Twitter, any other social network you'd like to show (or none if you prefer)

We use Markdown for the interviews, so it would be great if you used it too :). Otherwise, plain text is just fine.

So what did YOU use to build this website?

Well, curious creature, this site is actually open sourced on GitHub, where it's also hosted!

It's built using Jekyll, with a heavily modified "theme", using no more than the usual HTML/CSS (no preprocessors) and minimal JavaScript.
